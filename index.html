The Problem

Moving alone at night involves a quiet tension

Navigation apps optimize for time and distance — not for how a route feels. Many people, especially women and young adults, change their behavior: avoiding certain streets, pretending to be on a call, deciding not to walk at all. These situations rarely require emergency help, but they create ongoing stress and reduce independence.

The Solution

82 Confidence · Calm

A real-time Confidence Score for every route

CloseBy assigns every route a live score reflecting situational comfort and exposure risk. It recommends safer paths, favors active areas over empty ones, and flags unusual conditions as they develop. When reassurance is needed, an AI companion can simulate a phone call and — if the user becomes unresponsive — automatically notify trusted contacts with location.

How it works

Two layers, one number

A baseline layer estimates comfort from environmental signals: lighting, street activity, time of day. A real-time layer continuously updates the score through lightweight user reports — broken lighting, uncomfortable interactions, aggressive gatherings. Both combine into a single number you can see and trust.

Who it's for

Women and young adults moving alone in cities

Target users are people who navigate urban environments daily and while traveling. The personal safety app market is projected to reach $5.8B by 2032 — reflecting growing demand for tools that increase confidence in everyday mobility.
